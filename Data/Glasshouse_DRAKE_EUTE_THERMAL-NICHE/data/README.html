Description

Experiment: The GREAT experiment consisted of three provenances of Eucalyptus tereticornis (Temperate, Subtropical and Tropical) grown at six temperatures using controlled glasshouse bays in the S39 glasshouse at Western Sydney University from 2016-01-07 to 2016-02-29. The tropical provenance was also grown under well watered and water limited conditions in a drought sub-experiment. The goal was to document the fundamental thermal niche of these provenances, and to test whether the provenances differed in their temperature responses (generally speaking, they didn't). The files in this package contain data regarding tree growth, leaf-level photosynthesis (light response curves, temperature response curves, spot measurements), leaf respiration temperature response curves, leaf stem and root respiration rates at a common temperature, and environmental measurements.

Experiments

PCS- provenance climate shift

ID Experiment_17
Parent Facility Facility - Glasshouse S30
Description 9 provenances of Eucalyptus grandis and 12 provenances of E. tereticornis, spanning a latitudinal gradient in seed source from -38.0 to -15.5 degrees latitude, were grown at their "home" temperature regime and "home + 3.5 deg C". A suite of measurements related to growth, physiology, and molecular biology were collected to test the hypothesis that the degree of acclimation to warming is constrained in warm-origin taxa relative to cool-origin taxa. Also note, a subset of these provenances were used for an extensive drought and heatwave experiment.

Description Plant morphological measurements in the S30 glasshouse from a final harvest. Eucalyptus grandis and Eucalyptus tereticornis. Variables: code1: unique tree identifier code. First digit denotes species ("g" for grandis and "t" for tereticornis). Second digit denotes provenance (low letters like "b" are provenances from the south, while high letters "i" are from the north). Third digit denotes treatment ("h" for home, "w" for 3.5 deg C warming, or "r" for reciprocally transplanted). The last set of digits are numbers that denote the pot number. sp: species ("g" for grandis, "t" for teriticornis). prov: provenance treat: treatment ("h" for home, "w" for warmed, "r" for reciprocal). num: pot number ht: tree height (cm) diam: basal diameter at ~2cm height (mm) secondary_branches: the number of secondary branches branch_length: the average branch length (cm). Must have been measured on a subset? leafFM: total leaf fresh mass (g) tenleafFM: the fresh mass of a randomly selected ten-leaf subsample (g) tenleafarea: the total leaf area of the ten-leaf subsample (cm^2) tenleafDM: the dry mass of the ten-leaf subsample (g) leafDM: the dry mass of all canopy leaves (EXCEPT the ten-leaf subsample) (g). Note that you may want to add leafDM and tenleafDM to estimate total leaf dry mass. totalstemDM: the stem dry mass (g) rootDM: the root dry mass (g) totalDM: total plant dry mass (g)

GHS39_GREAT_MAIN_MET-AIR_20160107-20160302_L1.csv

Basic Information

Name GHS39_GREAT_MAIN_MET-AIR_20160107-20160302_L1.csv
Type PROCESSED
Size 36MB
File format text/plain
Description Experiment: The GREAT experiment consisted of three provenances of Eucalyptus tereticornis (Temperate, Subtropical and Tropical) grown at six temperatures using controlled glasshouse bays in the S39 glasshouse at Western Sydney University from 2016-01-07 to 2016-02-29. The tropical provenance was grown under well watered and water limited conditions. This file contains measurements of environmental data measured in the S39 glasshouse during the GREAT experiment, including air temperature, relative humidity, incident photosynthetic photon flux density, and vapor pressure deficit. Data were measured every second and minutely averages were recorded on the Campbell datalogger present in each bay. Note that these data reflect a different set of sensors than the sensors used for environmental control of the glasshouse bays. Note that trees were planted into the glasshouse bays on 2016-01-07 and the temperature treatments began on 2016-01-08. Thermal screens above the plants were deployed to reduce incident light until 2016-01-11; this was intended to reduce the chance of photo damage during the hot weekend just following planting. PAR sensors were only deployed in a subset of bays (3, 4, and 5) until 2016-2-04; PAR sensors were present in all bays after this date. Plants and temperature treatments were rotated across glasshouse bays on 2016-01-20 and 2016-01-21The experiment was terminated on 2016-03-02. Variable: Description: “Date” The date, in YYYY-MM-DD “DateTime” The date and time combination, in YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS “Bay” 3 to 8. The number of the physical glasshouse bay. "Room" 1 to 6. The temperature regime of the glasshouse room. Mean daily temperatures= 1: 18C, 2: 21.5C, 3: 25C, 4: 28.5C, 5: 32C and 6: 35.5C. Note that Bay and Room numbers were different, and their relationship changed when the plants were rotated in late Jan. “Tair” The air temperature, as measured by a shielded and aspirated sensor (degrees C) “RH” The relative humidity of the air, as measured by a shielded and aspirated sensor (%) “PAR” The incident photo flux density of photosynthetic active radiation (micromoles of photons m-2 s-1). Measured by a licor quantum sensors mounted on a metal pole placed near the center of each glasshouse bay. Care was taken to ensure that the sensor was level and above the leaves of surrounding trees, but this was not always possible. “VPD” The vapor pressure deficit (kPa), calculated from the Tair and RH variables.
